\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
We proposed a framework to characterize the attack surface of GSEs for poisoning attacks in the political domain, introducing content-injection barriers, domination scores, and citation grounding scores.
Experiments on three major GSEs in the US and Japan showed that (a) each model has a distinct attack surface determined by its citation selection strategy; (b) ruling parties have a broader attack surface than opposition parties; and (c) user profiles, one form of personalization, do not affect the attack surface, meaning the vulnerability depends on the model and the target party, not on the user.
