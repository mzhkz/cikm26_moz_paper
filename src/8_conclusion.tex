\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
We proposed a framework to characterize the potential attack surface of GSEs for poisoning attacks in the political domain, introducing content-injection barriers, domination scores, and citation grounding scores.
Experiments on three major GSEs in the US and Japan showed that (a) each model has a distinct attack surface determined by its citation selection strategy; (b) ruling parties have a broader attack surface than opposition parties; and (c) user profiles do not affect the potential attack surface, meaning the vulnerability depends on the model and the target party, not on the user.


\section*{Ethical Considerations}
Our study focuses on political questions, whose topics are sensitive yet increasingly important for discussing information delivery in democratic processes.
To minimize subjective bias, we adopt established political science frameworks (MARPOR) for question construction and select parties based on neutral structural criteria.
Our analysis characterizes the potential attack surface of citation patterns in GSEs but does not show actual attack cases to avoid facilitating attacks.
